philosophy
"Every brand problem is a story problem. The identity work and the narrative work are the same work.

Generative AI can create a thousand images in seconds. But it doesn't know why a child lies face-down in the grass, completely absorbed in an earthworm. It doesn't know why we keep texting numbers belonging to people who are gone.

We use intelligence to accelerate execution, but we rely on cinema to remember who we are."
thinking
Timing is sight
A story told too early feels premature. Told too late, it's a post-mortem. The mark of a good instinct is knowing which moment a culture is ready to hear something true. That's not prediction. That's listening.
Making is thinking
The script changes when you write it. The film changes when you direct it. Ideas live in abstraction until they hit material. Then they fight back. That resistance is where the real work starts.
Stories move people
A brand needs permission to speak. That permission comes from being honest about what problem it actually solves, what it believes, where it falls short. The narrative isn't decoration. It's the ground the brand stands on.
Tools are tools
Generative AI is fast. It's useful. But speed without intention is noise. The question isn't what the tool can do. It's what the story needs. The tool serves the story. Not the other way around.

The Last Message

screenplay / IP

What happens to the words people were saving for later, and never sent?

feb 2026 +
the seed
A conversation about grief. Someone mentioned they still texted their dead father's number sometimes. Not expecting a reply. Just because the habit had not stopped.
the question
What if the reply came?
the material

"This technology does not create the emotions people could not express. It just removes the excuse for not finding the words."

observation notes, 2025
News stories about grief tech startups. The quiet industry forming around loss and digital presence.
underneath
A story about the damage done by things left unsaid, and what it means when a service offers to say them for you.
the pivot
The first draft made the service the villain. That was too easy. The real question was not whether it was ethical. It was whether the son deserved to know which messages were real.

Registered screenplay. 18 pages. SWA India, Feb 2026.

Maati Kona Chi?

short film · marathi

Who decides what gets to grow, and what gets cut?

nov 2025 +
the seed
Watching a child lie flat on a garden, face inches from the earth, completely absorbed in an earthworm. The adult world moving around her, indifferent.
the question
Whose soil is it? And does the grass have a say?
the material

"Bichara gavat… vaadhlay ki khush hot asel."
(Poor grass… it must feel happy when it grows.)

Chinni, six years old, script excerpt
The idea that wildness is not disorder. That something growing beyond its boundary might simply be alive.
underneath
Belonging. Who inherits a place. What gets preserved and what gets managed out of existence.
the pivot
The script kept wanting to be written in English. Resisted it each time. The question only existed properly in Marathi. Some things cannot be translated without losing the argument.
the thing
Shooting spring 2026. 16mm. Marathi dialogue.
